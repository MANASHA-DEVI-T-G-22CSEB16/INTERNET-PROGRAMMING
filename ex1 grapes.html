ABOUT GRAPES
GRAPES
Botanical Classification:
  • Grapes belong to the genus Vitis.
  • The most common species used for winemaking and table grapes is Vitis vinifera.

Varieties:
  • There are thousands of grape varieties, each with its unique flavor, color, and use.
  • Common table grape varieties include Thompson Seedless, Red Globe, and Flame Seedless.
  • Wine grape varieties vary widely and include Cabernet Sauvignon, Chardonnay, Merlot, and many others.
Cultivation:
  • Grapes are grown in vineyards around the world, thriving in various climates.
  • They prefer well-drained soil and are often cultivated on trellises for support.
Health Benefits:
Grapes are rich in vitamins, particularly vitamin C and vitamin K.
They contain antioxidants, such as resveratrol, which may have heart-healthy benefits.
The skin of red grapes contains additional health-promoting compounds.

Products:
Wine: Grapes are a fundamental ingredient in wine production, with various grape varieties contributing to the diverse world of wines.
Juice: Grape juice is a popular beverage, either fresh or processed into concentrate.
Raisins: Dried grapes, or raisins, are a sweet and nutritious snack.
