ABOUT STRAWBERRY

Botanical Information:
Scientific Name: Fragaria × ananassa
Family: Rosaceae

Origins and History:

  • Strawberries have a long history and are believed to have been cultivated for thousands of years.
  • The modern cultivated strawberry, Fragaria × ananassa, is a hybrid of two wild strawberry species from North America and Chile
Seasonality:
  • Strawberries are generally in season during the spring and early summer, depending on the region and the specific variety.
Culinary Uses:
  • Strawberries are versatile and can be enjoyed fresh, sliced, or in various dishes.
  • They are commonly used in desserts such as cakes, pies, and ice cream.
  • Strawberry jams and preserves are popular spreads.
Harvesting:
  • Strawberries are typically harvested when they reach full ripeness, which is when they have a bright red color and are firm to the touch.
  • They are often picked by hand to avoid bruising the delicate fruit.
Popular Varieties:
  • There are numerous strawberry varieties, each with its own unique flavor, size, and characteristics.
  • Some popular varieties include 'Chandler,' 'Albion,' and 'Elsanta.'
